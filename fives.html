a5 × b5

Sometimes, using this trick can be much faster than using the FOILing method in multiplying numbers.

Let a5 and b5 be two two-digit numbers such that a5 = a×10+5 and b5 = b×10+5.

Then a5×b5 = (a×10+5)×(b×10+5) = 100(a×b) + 50×(a+b) + 25

= 100(a×b+(a+b)÷2) + 25

We have two cases for the last two digits of the answer.

If (a+b) % 2 = 0, then the last two digits are 25.

If (a+b) % 2 = 1, then the last two digits are 75.

Then the rest of the answer is a×b+⌊(a+b)÷2⌋ where the floor of a number is the greatest integer that is less than or equal to the number.

Example: 35×65

We have a = 3 and b = 6. Then a+b is 9 since 3 + 6 = 9. Since 9 is an odd number, the last two digits of the answer is 75.

We then find the other digits by using the formula a×b+⌊(a+b)÷2⌋.

a×b+⌊(a+b)÷2⌋ = 3×6+⌊(9)÷2⌋ = 18+4 (since the floor of 9÷2 is 4) = 22.

The answer to 35×65 is 2275.

Example: 45×85

We have a = 4 and b = 8. Then a+b is 12 since 4 + 8 = 12. Since 12 is an even number, the last two digits of the answer is 25.

We then find the other digits by using the formula a×b+⌊(a+b)÷2⌋.

a×b+⌊(a+b)÷2⌋ = 4×8+⌊(12)÷2⌋ = 32+6 (since the floor of 6 is 6) = 38.

The answer to 45×85 is 3825.
