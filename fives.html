a5 × b5

Sometimes, using this trick can be much faster than using the FOILing method in multiplying numbers.

Let a5 and b5 be two two-digit numbers such that a5 = a×10+5 and b5 = b×10+5.

Then a5×b5 = (a×10+5)×(b×10+5) = 100(a×b) + 50×(a+b) + 25

= 100(a×b+(a+b)÷2) + 25

We have two cases for the last two digits of the answer.

If (a+b) % 2 = 0, then the last two digits are 25.

If (a+b) % 2 = 1, then the last two digits are 75.

Then the rest of the answer is a×b+⌊(a+b)÷2⌋ where the floor of a number is the greatest integer that is less than or equal to the number.
